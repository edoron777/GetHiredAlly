STAR Method - How to Answer Behavioral Questions
When interviewers ask "Tell me about a time when...", use STAR:

S - SITUATION: Set the scene. Where were you? What was the context? T - TASK: What was your responsibility? What needed to be done? A - ACTION: What did YOU specifically do? (Use "I", not "we") R - RESULT: What happened? Quantify if possible (%, $, time saved)

Example Structure: "In my previous role at [Company], we faced [Situation]. My task was to [Task]. I decided to [Action], specifically by [details]. As a result, [Result with numbers if possible]."

Tip: Prepare 3-5 STAR stories that can be adapted to different questions.

Story Prompts:

Stakeholder Management: Tell me about a time you had to manage conflicting priorities between different teams (e.g., R&D vs. Sales).
Communication: Describe a situation where you had to explain a complex technical concept to a non-technical audience.
Strategic Thinking: Tell me about a time you identified a new market opportunity and developed a product strategy to capitalize on it.
